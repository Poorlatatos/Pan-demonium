**Pan-Fried Gyoza**

Japanese Dumplings with a Crispy Bottom

Crispy on the bottom and juicy on the inside, these homemade gyoza are a staple in Japanese cuisine. Enjoy this easy-to-follow recipe to create restaurant-quality dumplings at home!

Prep Time: 30 mins | Cook Time: 10 mins | Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 30 gyoza wrappers
  • 1/2 lb ground pork (or chicken)
  • 1 cup napa cabbage, finely chopped
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1/4 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the filling: In a bowl, combine ground pork, cabbage, green onions, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  2. Fill the wrappers: Place 1 teaspoon of filling in the center of each gyoza wrapper. Moisten the edges with water, then fold and pleat to seal.
  3. Cook the gyoza: Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Place gyoza flat side down in a single layer and cook until the bottom turns golden brown (about 2-3 minutes).
  4. Steam: Add 1/4 cup of water to the pan, cover with a lid, and steam for 5 minutes or until the wrappers are tender.
  5. Finish and serve: Remove the lid and let the remaining water evaporate. Serve hot with soy sauce or dipping sauce.

Enjoy your crispy and juicy pan-fried gyoza with a side of dipping sauce made of soy sauce, rice vinegar, and chili oil!
