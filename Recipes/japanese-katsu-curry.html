**Japanese Katsu Curry**

A Crispy and Flavorful Classic with Rich Curry Sauce

A beloved Japanese comfort dish, Katsu Curry combines crispy breaded pork (or chicken) cutlet with a thick, aromatic curry sauce, served over fluffy rice. Perfectly balanced flavors make this a satisfying meal!

Prep Time: 20 mins | Cook Time: 30 mins | Serves: 2-4

Ingredients:

For the Curry Sauce:

  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tbsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Katsu:

  • 2 boneless pork chops or chicken breasts
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

For Serving:

  • 2 cups cooked Japanese short-grain rice
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped (optional)
  • Fukujinzuke (Japanese pickles) for garnish

Instructions:

Making the Curry Sauce:

  1. Sauté the vegetables: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add onions and carrots, cooking until softened.
  2. Add garlic and spices: Stir in garlic, curry powder, and garam masala, cooking for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Thicken the sauce: Sprinkle flour over the mixture, stirring continuously for another minute.
  4. Simmer: Gradually add broth while stirring, then mix in soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, honey, and grated ginger.
  5. Cook: Let simmer for 15-20 minutes until the sauce thickens. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Preparing the Katsu:

  1. Flatten the meat: Gently pound pork or chicken to an even thickness.
  2. Season: Sprinkle with salt and pepper.
  3. Bread the cutlets: Dredge in flour, dip into beaten egg, and coat with panko breadcrumbs.
  4. Fry: Heat oil in a pan to 350°F (175°C) and fry cutlets for 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Drain: Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to remove excess oil.
  6. Slice: Cut into strips for serving.

Assembling the Dish:

  1. Plate the rice: Serve a portion of cooked rice on a plate.
  2. Add the katsu: Place the sliced katsu cutlet on top of or next to the rice.
  3. Pour the curry: Spoon the hot curry sauce over the katsu and rice.
  4. Garnish: Sprinkle with chopped green onions and add a side of Fukujinzuke if desired.

Enjoy your homemade Japanese Katsu Curry—a perfect blend of crunch, spice, and rich umami flavors!
