**Beef Wellington**

A Luxurious and Flavorful Masterpiece

Beef Wellington is the ultimate indulgence—a tender beef fillet coated in savory mushroom duxelles, wrapped in prosciutto and golden, flaky puff pastry. Perfect for special occasions or an elegant dinner.

Prep Time: 30 mins | Chill Time: 30 mins | Cook Time: 40 mins | Serves: 4

Ingredients:

For the Beef:

  • 1 (1.5 lb) beef tenderloin
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard

For the Mushroom Duxelles:

  • 8 oz cremini or button mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 shallot, finely diced
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

For Assembly:

  • 6 slices prosciutto
  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 egg yolk, beaten (for egg wash)
  • All-purpose flour (for dusting)

Instructions:

Preparing the Beef:

  1. Season the tenderloin: Generously season the beef with salt and black pepper.
  2. Sear the beef: Heat olive oil in a pan over high heat and sear the beef on all sides until golden brown (about 2 minutes per side). Remove from heat and brush all over with Dijon mustard. Let cool.

Making the Mushroom Duxelles:

  1. Sauté the mushrooms: In the same pan, melt butter over medium heat. Add mushrooms, shallots, garlic, and thyme. Cook until all moisture evaporates and mixture becomes paste-like (about 10 minutes).
  2. Season: Add salt and pepper to taste. Let cool.

Assembling the Wellington:

  1. Lay out the prosciutto: On a sheet of plastic wrap, arrange the prosciutto slices overlapping slightly.
  2. Spread the mushroom duxelles: Evenly spread the mushroom mixture over the prosciutto.
  3. Wrap the beef: Place the beef tenderloin on top and tightly roll everything together using the plastic wrap. Chill for 30 minutes to set the shape.

Wrapping in Puff Pastry:

  1. Roll out the puff pastry: On a floured surface, roll out the pastry slightly larger than the wrapped beef.
  2. Encase the beef: Remove the plastic wrap from the beef and place it in the center of the pastry. Fold over and seal the edges tightly.
  3. Brush with egg wash: Brush the pastry with beaten egg yolk for a golden finish.
  4. Chill again: Refrigerate for 10 minutes while preheating the oven to 400°F (200°C).

Baking and Serving:

  1. Bake: Place the wrapped beef on a baking sheet and bake for 35-40 minutes until golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 125°F (52°C) for medium-rare.
  2. Rest: Let the Wellington rest for 10 minutes before slicing.
  3. Serve: Slice into thick portions and enjoy with a side of roasted vegetables or a rich red wine sauce.

Savor the rich, buttery layers of puff pastry, the earthy depth of mushrooms, and the juicy tenderness of perfectly cooked beef—Beef Wellington at its finest!
